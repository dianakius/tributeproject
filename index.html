Life of Etta James

Etta James (1938–2012) — Legendary American singer whose powerful voice bridged blues, soul, jazz, and rock. Known for timeless hits like "At Last", she overcame personal struggles to become one of music’s most celebrated and influential artists.

Here's the timeline of her life:

    1938–1959: Childhood and Career Beginnings

  • Born: January 25, 1938, Los Angeles, CA, to a 14-year-old mother; father unidentified (possibly "Minnesota Fats").
  • Upbringing: Raised in foster homes and by her grandparents; began singing gospel at age 5 under James Earle Hines.
  • Trauma: Endured abuse from foster figures, leading to anxiety performing on demand later in life.
  • Move to San Francisco: After foster mother Mama Lu died in 1950, she moved with her birth mother to the Fillmore district.
  • Formed the Creolettes: A girl group inspired by doo-wop.
  • Discovered by Johnny Otis: Renamed “Etta James”; Creolettes became “The Peaches”.
  • Breakthrough Hit: “The Wallflower” (“Roll with Me, Henry”) hit #1 on R&B chart in 1955.
  • Solo Career Begins: Left The Peaches; had another R&B hit with "Good Rockin' Daddy".
  • Romantic Links: Involved with B.B. King and Harvey Fuqua.
